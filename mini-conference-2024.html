COSMOS Mini-Conference 2024

On April 12–13, 2024, COSMOS and the OSU School of Communication hosted the inaugural computational communication research mini-conference, bringing together scholars to explore measurement challenges and digital-era questions in health, science, and political communication.

At a glance

Dates: April 12–13, 2024

Location: Pomerene Hall, Ohio State University

Format: Public research talks on Friday and an invite-only workshop on Saturday

Participants: OSU School of Communication faculty and students, along with colleagues from Political Science, Sociology, and Psychology

What we covered

  • Measurement challenges in computational communication research.
  • Societal challenges in health, science, and political communication.
  • Digital news, platforms, and information flows across communities.
  • Collaborative discussions and planning for future projects.

Featured speakers

  • Kaiping Chen (University of Wisconsin–Madison)
  • Sandra González-Bailón (University of Pennsylvania)
  • Jihye Lee (University of Texas at Austin)
  • Yingdan Lu (Northwestern University)
  • Katherine Ognyanova (Rutgers University)
  • Jakob Ohme (Weizenbaum Institute)
  • Winson Peng (Michigan State University)
  • Jingwen Zhang (University of California, Davis)

Highlights

Friday public program

A full day of panels and discussion focused on computational measurement, societal challenges, and digital news dynamics. The event concluded with a lab tour and dinner to continue the conversation.

Saturday workshop

An invite-only working session with shorter presentations and round-table discussions designed to deepen collaborations and identify shared research opportunities.

With thanks

We are grateful for the support of the OSU School of Communication and the Translational Data Analytics Institute, whose partnership made the mini-conference possible.

The event was a lot of fun, and we learned a great deal from our guests. We look forward to hosting outside speakers and another mini-conference soon.
